About

Pavel Teimer, a Czech typographer and graphic designer, designed a modern serif roman with italics in 1967, drawing inspiration from Walbaum and Didot rather than Bodoni. Teimer's antiqua featured softer, livelier characters with less contrast and wider italics. Though submitted to the Czechoslovak Grafotechna type foundry, it was never cast. In 2005, Suitcase Type Foundry, led by Tomáš Brousil, digitized and expanded the typeface, modifying some characters and adding new glyphs. Released in 2006 as Teimer Std, the family now includes 24 styles ranging from light to bold.
